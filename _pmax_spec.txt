Here is a detailed explanation of the **NWP Newport PMAX (Maximum Power Protection) feature**, including architecture, throttle levels, hardware, timing, register/BIOS controls, DFX, and NWP/DMR differences:

---

## **1. MT-PMax Architecture**

- **Multi-Threshold PMAX (MT-PMax):**  
  - Monitors VccIN (FIVR input) voltage using an on-die analog detector.
  - Compares VccIN against **three independently fuse-programmable thresholds** (Vtrip0, Vtrip1, Vtrip2) for progressive (soft) throttling, plus a fourth threshold for ** FDP**.
  - Each threshold triggers a different level of throttling response, with the highest (Vtrip2) corresponding to a hard throttle event.

---

## **2. Throttle Levels (Vtrip 0/1/2/3)**

- **Vtrip0, Vtrip1:**  
  - Lower thresholds, typically used for **soft/proportional throttling** (e.g., PWM-based, frequency clipping).
  - Intended to reduce power before a hard throttle is needed.
- **Vtrip2:**  
  - Highest programmable threshold, triggers **hard throttle** (immediate, aggressive frequency/power reduction).
- **Vtrip3 (FDP):**  
  - Dedicated to **Fast Droop Protection**—detects rapid voltage droops to protect FIVR Vmin.

- **Encoding:**  
  - Trip levels are encoded in fuses; 1 code = 2mV VccIN (mapping to IccIN via loadline).

---

## **3. Hardware Detector Locations**

- **NWP Newport:**  
  - **imh0 (NIO die):** Houses the main PMAX detector circuitry.
  - **cbb0, cbb1:** Receive throttle signals from imh0; no independent PMAX detectors.
- **DMR:**  
  - Dual VCCIN domains, with PMAX detectors on both IMH dies (north/south partitions).

---

## **4. D2D Fast-Throttle Wire**

- **YY_FAST_THROTTLE_IN_0:**  
  - Dedicated die-to-die (D2D) wire for **hard throttle** signaling from imh0 to cbb0/cbb1.
  - Ensures **sub-microsecond response** (target: <2μs, typically ~50ns for power reduction).
- **YY_FAST_THROTTLE_IN_1:**  
  - Used for PWM/proportional throttling (soft throttle).

---

## **5. FIVR VccIN Sensing**

- **PMAX detector** directly senses the FIVR input voltage (VccIN) at imh0.
- ** FDP:**  
  - Additional logic in PMAX IP for rapid detection of large voltage droops, with direct signaling to core throttle logic.

---

## **6. PMax Frequency Response Timing**

- **Hard throttle (Vtrip2):**  
  - Power reduction to Psafe must occur within **50ns** of D2D wire assertion.
- **Soft throttle (Vtrip0/1):**  
  - Proportional throttling is engaged as VccIN approaches higher thresholds, slowing the voltage drop and reducing the likelihood of hard throttle.
- **FDP:**  
  - Response loop (detection to throttle) is completed within the first droop time constant (nanoseconds).

---

## **7. Register Interface for PMax Configuration**

- **PMAX_CONTROL (imh0):**  
  - Programs trip thresholds, enables/disables PMAX, masks outputs.
- **ICCMAX_CONFIG:**  
  - Allows Pcode to adjust trip levels dynamically (e.g., for IccMax reduction).
- **GPIO PMAX_TRIGGER_IO:**  
  - Bi-directional IO for external Pmax event injection or live observability (configurable as RX/TX/disabled via BIOS).
- **Punit/PrimeCode registers:**  
  - Masking PMAX during package C6, clearing/resetting throttle events, and configuring edge/level retrigger logic.

---

## **8. BIOS Knob Controls**

- **PMAX_TRIGGER_IO mode:**  
  - BIOS can set to RX (default, for platform-driven events), TX (for validation/DFX), or disabled.
- **Trip threshold adjustment:**  
  - BIOS can adjust hard throttle trip threshold (legacy capability).
- **Feature disable:**  
  - Overclocking platforms may disable PMAX for higher performance.

---

## **9. NWP-Specific Deltas from DMR**

- **Single VCCIN on NIO (imh0):**  
  - NWP has a single VCCIN domain and PMAX detector on imh0.
  - DMR-AP has dual VCCIN domains, with PMAX detectors on both IMH dies.
- **Throttle signaling:**  
  - NWP: imh0 drives D2D fast-throttle wire to both cbb0 and cbb1.
  - DMR: Each IMH partition independently manages its CBBs.
- **Simplified coordination:**  
  - No need for cross-die HPM messaging for Pmax events in NWP.

---

## **10. DFX Observability for PMax Events**

- **PMAX_TRIGGER_IO GPIO:**  
  - In TX mode, allows external monitoring of live PMAX events (scope/SMB connector).
- **Registers:**  
  - Event counters and status bits in PMAX IP and Punit for validation.
- **FDP event counters:**  
  - Registers track number of FDP triggers for validation and debug.

---

## **11. Risks and Corner Cases**

- **Platform voltage tolerance:**  
  - Mapping of IccMax reduction to trip codes is sensitive to loadline and voltage accuracy.
- **PkgC6 interaction:**  
  - PMAX must be masked/disabled during deep package C-states to avoid false triggers.
- **External event injection:**  
  - If PMAX_TRIGGER_IO is misconfigured, external events could cause unintended throttling.
- **Overclocking:**  
  - Disabling PMAX increases risk of Vmin violations and FIVR instability.
- **Illegal trip encoding:**  
  - Only 001, 011, 111 are valid; 010, 110, 100 are illegal and must be avoided.

---

## **Summary Table**

| Aspect                | NWP Newport (NIO)                | DMR-AP                        |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| VCCIN domains         | Single (imh0)                    | Dual (IMH0, IMH1)             |
| PMAX detector         | imh0 only                        | Both IMH dies                 |

| Trip levels           | 3 soft + 1 FDP                   | 3 soft + 1 FDP                |
| GPIO observability    | PMAX_TRIGGER_IO (bi-dir, BIOS)   | PMAX_TRIGGER_IO               |

| DFX                   | GPIO, event counters             | GPIO, event counters          |
